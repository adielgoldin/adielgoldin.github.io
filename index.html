About myself

Welcome to my website! I hold an L.L.B. degree in Global Law from Tilburg University, specializing in commercial and transnational litigation. Understanding the intricate, multi-jurisdictional aspects of today's global market is crucial for effective legal practice.

With a solid academic foundation, I've further honed my skills through internships in a law firm and the in-house legal department of a satellite company in Singapore. These experiences have deepened my understanding of real-world legal dynamics and strengthened my ability to apply theoretical knowledge to practical scenarios.

This portfolio contains a selection of papers and projects that I have worked on, showcasing my legal skills and knowledge. From term sheets to compliance matters, these works demonstrate my ability to research, analyze, and communicate complex legal concepts effectively.

Platform Workers: Employees, Or Not, And Why Does It Matter?

The article discusses the classification of workers in the gig economy, focusing on platforms like Uber, Airbnb, and Just Eat. It delves into the legal distinctions between employees and contractors, highlighting the significance of this classification in terms of rights and benefits. The analysis covers the approaches of the U.S., the UK, and the EU, outlining their criteria for defining platform workers and shedding light on the ongoing debates and potential future approaches in this evolving landscape. The article underscores the importance of this classification, as it impacts workers, platforms, and customers within the gig economy.
